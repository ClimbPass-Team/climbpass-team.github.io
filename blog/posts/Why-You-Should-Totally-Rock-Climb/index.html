Why You Should Totally Rock Climb


1) It is a great way to keep fit and healthy.

Maybe you’re bored of running or skipping. Maybe you’re intending to leave your couch potato reputation behind. Either way, you should really consider rock climbing!

It’s a full body work-out: engaging your core, building your arm muscles and increasing your lower body strength. It improves your cardiovascular health. It’s almost like a rowing machine, incline bench and treadmill rolled into one perfect package.

In a society that prizes efficiency, isn’t this a fabulous deal?





2) It trains your problem-solving skills.

Perhaps your initial impression of rock climbing is simply spotting and grabbing the handhold nearest to you and lifting yourself to the top with pure brute strength. The truth, however, isn’t that straightforward! But that’s what makes it exciting and fresh all the time.

Like an explorer you’ll learn to examine the layout of the wall, identifying the “problems” and then coming up with a rough plan on getting to your destination. There’s no “model” route to get to the top; the path you takes depends on your size, skill, strength and smarts.

How should I position my body, where should I rest my feet, which edge should I reach out to first…it’s all about calculated moves to find the optimum path using the least effort.





3) It gives you a sense of accomplishment.

You’ve probably watched a climber reach the top once and thought to yourself, wow, I’m not sure if I can do that…

Well, the thing is, with proper training, practice and grit, that isn’t impossible.

Climbing routes have varying difficulty levels and you can choose one that fits your current capabilities so that it’s not too easy to make you feel bored nor too challenging to crush your spirits from the get-go.

Every tiny improvement you make, every inch you climb and every milliseconds you cut off from your climb counts. It’s an activity that is rather easy to set up goals and work towards achieving them!





4) It improves your kinesthetic sense.

Bid your two left feet goodbye! Since rock climbing requires much spatial awareness and ocular-motor coordination, you’ll eventually train your mind and body to have better balance and move more gracefully.

Rock climbing ain’t just for buffing up; it’s time to move with style and suaveness.





5) It is a social sport.

It’s a great opportunity to meet people from diverse backgrounds in the climbing gyms. Go there enough times and you’ll be bound to see familiar faces.

Like a bench presser and his/her spotter, the relationship you’ll build with your climbing partner/belayer over time is one of trust and confidence.

You may even bring your friends and family with you to cheer or join you!





6) It is a solo sport.

Aiming for some sweet alone time to sweat it out or engage in your own thoughts? You don’t need a belayer if you’re bouldering. All you need is a rock wall and then me, myself and I.

Unlike a group activity like soccer, net ball or water polo, you don’t need to make schedule arrangements with others when it comes to bouldering.

This means swinging by the climbing gyms anytime during their opening hours or lazing away on the couch at home, whatever your whims. Perfect for introverts or those who dread timetables.





7) You look rad in pictures.

You’re already clinging on to a gorgeous canvas, be it nature’s creation or a man-made wall speckled with colourful rocks.

Add in good lighting, climbing gear and angle, and every photo of you will simply ooze out coolness, fun and adventure by default. Skip the filters—you don’t need them.

It makes for good memories on Instagram or your family’s photo album.





8) The view up there is amazing.

Sure, you may live on the 24th floor and look out of your window every day, but this is different. Your surroundings have never been so vast, so open, so unique.

You’re seeing the gym, the mall, the area, the space from a vintage that would have never happened if it weren’t for the people on the ground ensuring your safety, the technology used to build the equipment holding you, and your grit in bringing yourself up there.

That’s a view worth savouring.

You Know You’re Deeply into Rock Climbing when…

You hook carabiners on everything. The greatest tool, fashion and life hack ever.

You own more chalk than early 20th century Maths Professors.

No one in your family gets that baby powder and chalk are vastly different.

Most of your possessions are covered with white powder. The non-illegal kind, obviously.

You’re an expert in pantomiming.

Touch screens are a bane because your hands are so calloused.





You used to buy size 8 shoes. Now you go for a size 7.

Rice water doesn’t save your hands anymore.

Those are not punk rock ballet shoes, Mum.

Filing your finger nails and trimming your toe nails is the new therapeutic session.

Even if you’re a total beginner, you love the aura of ruggedness surrounding you.

You worship Catherine Destivelle, Chris Sharma and Steph Davis etc.

You catch yourself fondling surfaces sometimes.

You almost broke an ankle. Or pulled a shoulder.





You can relate to the women of Tang Dynasty who had undergone foot binding.

You have your own mental catalogue of adjectives describing the texture of surfaces you touch. This rock feels like an orange peel pulled over a deformed clay sculpture.

During the first few days of Circuit Breaker, you may have cried a little when you heard that the climbing gyms would be closed.

Your storeroom is mostly filled with climbing equipment and toilet paper.

Or the equipment is kept in your bedroom cupboard.

Or on your bedside table.

Or bed.





You stare at a piece of architecture, immediately examining how climbable it is.

If there was a flood or fire, you already have your escape plan.

You start finding holds everywhere, admiring the evolution of your digits.

If only you had toe pads like a gecko.

You see cracks and imagine how your body fits there.

The only thing your sleeping position reveals about your personality is that you want to avoid tennis elbows and neck kinks. (Though sometimes you go against Doctor’s orders.)





You find yourself studying intricate knot patterns you see sometimes.

You live for large pockets, many pockets. The design of ladies’ shorts and trousers appalls you.

You are strongly aware of objects within your reach.

Lava Floor has become your next favourite game in your spare time.

If you were a Boy Scout or Girl Guide, you would ace their knot tying assessments.

You low key think that rock climbing is the coolest sport out there, hands down.

What are the new habits you form or things you realise as you get deeper into rock climbing?
